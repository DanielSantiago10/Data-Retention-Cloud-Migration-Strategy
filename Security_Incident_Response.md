## 1. Executive Summary
Cymbal Bank is currently operating at a critical threshold regarding data storage and regulatory compliance. Due to hardware funding constraints, we lack a formal data retention policy, which has resulted in our on-premises servers nearing **100% capacity**. 

This proposal outlines a cost-effective transition to a **Hybrid Cloud model**. By implementing a structured retention policy and leveraging scalable cloud storage, we can mitigate significant legal risks and eliminate the need for immediate, high-cost physical hardware investments.

## 3. Proposed Mitigation Strategy (10-Week Roadmap)
We will follow a disciplined, three-phase approach to resolve these vulnerabilities.



### Phase 1: Policy Governance (Weeks 1-5)
Establish a formal **Retention Schedule** categorized by data type (Structured vs. Unstructured). We will align with legal counsel to define "Hot" (active) and "Cold" (archival) data lifecycles.

### Phase 2: Cloud Integration (Weeks 6-8)
Transition archival data to a **Public Cloud** environment. This shift moves our infrastructure costs from **CapEx** (Capital Expenditure) to a flexible **OpEx** (Operational Expenditure) model. 
* *Primary focus:* Data encryption, access controls, and redundancy across multiple regions.

### Phase 3: Organizational Alignment (Weeks 9-10)
Comprehensive staff training on new data handling standards. We will implement audit logs to ensure ongoing compliance and adjust policies based on operational feedback.

---

## 4. Strategic Recommendations
To ensure long-term sustainability and data integrity, I recommend the following:
1. **Adopt a Tiered Storage Model:** Use high-performance storage for active banking and low-cost archival tiers for compliance data.
2. **Formalize Access Controls:** Utilize **Identity & Access Management (IAM)** to ensure data is only accessible to authorized personnel.
3. **Annual Policy Audits:** Review and update retention schedules yearly to adapt to changing international data laws.
